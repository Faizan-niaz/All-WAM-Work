Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and the current Prime Minister of Pakistan. He was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan. Khan is the founder and leader of the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, which is one of the major political parties in Pakistan. Before entering politics, Imran Khan had a successful career in cricket. He captained the Pakistan national cricket team and led them to their first-ever Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. He is considered one of the greatest cricketers in Pakistan's history.

In the political realm, Khan emerged as a prominent figure advocating for social justice and anti-corruption. He founded PTI in 1996 and gradually gained popularity for his strong stance against corruption and his promises to reform the country's governance. After several unsuccessful attempts, Imran Khan's party achieved a significant breakthrough in the 2018 general elections, securing the most seats in the National Assembly. Consequently, he became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan on August 18, 2018.
